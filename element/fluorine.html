FLUORINE

Fluorine is a highly reactive and corrosive element. It is a pale yellow gas at room temperature.
It is used in various chemical processes and in the production of fluorinated compounds.
🧪
Chemical Properties
Fluorine is highly reactive, forming compounds with almost all other elements. It reacts violently with water.
💣
Physical Properties
Fluorine has a boiling point of -188.12°C and a melting point of -219.67°C.
🎯
Uses
Fluorine is used in the production of Teflon, uranium hexafluoride, and refrigerants.
