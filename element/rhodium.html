RHODIUM

Rhodium is a chemical element with the symbol Rh and atomic number 45.
It is a rare, silvery-white, hard, and chemically inert transition metal.
🧪
Chemical Properties
Rhodium is resistant to corrosion and oxidation. It is not attacked by most acids but dissolves in hot concentrated sulfuric acid and can be attacked by chlorine gas at high temperatures.
💣
Physical Properties
Rhodium is a hard, durable metal with a high melting point of 1964°C and a boiling point of 3695°C. It has a shiny, silvery appearance and is reflective.
🎯
Uses
Rhodium is primarily used in catalytic converters for automobiles to reduce harmful emissions. It is also used in jewelry plating, electrical contacts, and as a catalyst in the chemical industry.
