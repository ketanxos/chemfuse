TERBIUM

Terbium is a chemical element with the symbol Tb and atomic number 65.
It is a silvery-white rare earth metal that is malleable, ductile, and soft enough to be cut with a knife.
🧪
Chemical Properties
Terbium is relatively stable in air but can burn easily to form terbium(III) oxide. It reacts with water, evolving hydrogen gas.
💣
Physical Properties
Terbium is a silvery-white metal that is soft and malleable. It has a melting point of 1,356°C and a boiling point of 3,230°C.
🎯
Uses
Terbium is used in solid-state devices, as a dopant in calcium fluoride, and in the production of green phosphors for color TV tubes and LED lights.
