HOLMIUM

Holmium is a rare earth element with the symbol Ho and atomic number 67.
It is part of the lanthanide series and is known for its high magnetic strength.
🧪
Chemical Properties
Holmium reacts slowly with oxygen and water, and quickly dissolves in acids. It forms compounds such as holmium oxide (Ho2O3) and holmium chloride (HoCl3).
💣
Physical Properties
Holmium is a silvery-white metal with a melting point of 1474°C and a boiling point of 2700°C. It is highly magnetic.
🎯
Uses
Holmium is used in laser materials, magnets, and nuclear control rods due to its high magnetic strength and neutron-absorbing properties.
