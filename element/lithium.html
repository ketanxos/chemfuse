LITHIUM

Lithium is a soft, silvery-white metal and is the lightest metal and the least dense solid element.
It is used in rechargeable batteries for mobile phones, laptops, digital cameras, and electric vehicles.
🧪
Chemical Properties
Lithium reacts slowly with water to form lithium hydroxide and hydrogen gas.
💣
Physical Properties
Lithium has a boiling point of 1590°C and a melting point of 180.5°C.
🎯
Uses
Lithium is used in rechargeable batteries, in heat-resistant glass and ceramics, and in certain medications for bipolar disorder.
